Let’s examine the Hospital Charge Dataset from data.gov

I wanted to analyse this dataset because I wanted to know the different charges among different providers

The dataset has the following columns:

  • DRG Definition (diagnosis)
  • Provider Id
  • Provider Name
  • Provider Street Address
  • Provider City
  • Provider State
  • Provider Zip Code
  • Hospital Referral Region Description
  • Total Discharges
  • Average Covered Charges
  • Average Total Payments
  • Average Medicare Payments

The Average Covered Charges differs with differing providers. This will be our column of interest. Let us take a look at its histogram after converting the column to a float. We must remember diagnoses have been clubbed together to allow for easy coding and charging. However, the granularity of individual disease is lost because of this.

Histogram of the Average Charge

We see a few outliers beyond the 250000 mark. Let’s explore who these might be. There are apparently 513 instances of charges above that.

The top 5 diagnoses for this outlier are:

  • 870 SEPTICEMIA OR SEVERE SEPSIS W MV 96+ HOURS 132
  • 329 MAJOR SMALL & LARGE BOWEL PROCEDURES W MCC 117
  • 853 INFECTIOUS & PARASITIC DISEASES W O.R. PROCEDURE W MCC 117
  • 207 RESPIRATORY SYSTEM DIAGNOSIS W VENTILATOR SUPPORT 96+ HOURS 96
  • 460 SPINAL FUSION EXCEPT CERVICAL W/O MCC 15

Looks like septicemia or severe sepsis with mechanical ventilation is an expensive disease to treat. Mechanical ventilation means that a tube has been inserted a person’s windpipe and a machine helps with his breathing. Understandably, this is only done in ICU and is expensive.
